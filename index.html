Core Expertise

Computational Methods

We develop and apply state-of-the-art simulation techniques across quantum, atomistic, and mesoscopic scales. Our open-source tools and validated protocols enable reproducible nanoscale modeling.

Quantum Scale

Density Functional Theory DFT

Ground-state electronic structure, total energy calculations, geometry optimization

Time-Dependent DFT TD-DFT

Excited states, optical properties, ultrafast dynamics

Density Functional Perturbation Theory DFPT

Phonons, dielectric response, electron-phonon coupling

Atomistic Scale

Molecular Dynamics MD

Classical and ab initio MD, thermal transport, mechanical properties

Tight-Binding Method TB

Large-scale electronic structure, transport in nanostructures

Transport & Mesoscale

Nonequilibrium Green's Function NEGF

Quantum transport, current-voltage characteristics, nanodevice simulation

Three Pillars of Open Nano

We're building infrastructure to remove barriers and accelerate discovery in nanotechnology research.

Open by Default

All research outputs, data, and protocols are openly licensed and immediately accessible. No paywalls, no embargoes, no exceptions.

Active Project

Nano Characterization Database

Building an open-access repository for standardized nanomaterial characterization data, including AFM, TEM, and spectroscopy datasets with full provenance tracking.

0

Why This Matters

Nanotechnology suffers from irreproducibility due to inconsistent reporting and isolated data silos. We're fixing that.

  • Standardized metadata schemas for all major characterization techniques
  • Version-controlled protocols with community validation
  • Integration with existing repositories (Zenodo, Figshare, OSF)
  • API access for automated data harvesting
